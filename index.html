About

I am currently an Associate Scientist at the Centre of Remote Sensing & Image Processing at NUS and mainly focus on the fields of computer vision, image processing and machine learning. Specifically, I am interested in their applications on real world problems such as solar irradiance forecasting, cloud motion prediction and wind velocity prediction to name a few.

I have recently graduated from the University of Cambridge where I was an Angela-Dunn Gardner Scholar at Girton College and am looking to pursue a PhD in the coming few months. Apart from the aforementioned research fields, in my free time I also maintain an interest in areas related to astrophysics and, in particular, exoplanetary habitability.

1. Solar Irradiation Prediction



By utilizing images obtained from the Himawari 8 satellite, we aim to obtain a solar irradiance forecast for a 1-3 hour timeframe with a spatial resolution of around 4 km. One of the important steps is to distinguish cloud pixels from other features, and to achieve this we employ a combination of physical methods and machine learning techniques such as the Random Forest approach. The figure above shows the result of our cloud segmentation algortihm.

2. Cloud Position Forecast



Another active research area is in cloud tracking for the intra-hour time frame. By coupling various computer vision techniques and data from ground-based sky imaging systems, my research group aims to predict future cloud positions at a spatial and temporal resolution that is higher than currently achievable resolutions.
